Computer Technology Program

Program Overview

Our Computer Technology program provides hands-on training in both hardware and software aspects of modern computing systems.

Curriculum Highlights

  • Computer hardware assembly and maintenance
  • Operating system installation and configuration

Welding Technology Program

Master metal fabrication techniques for careers in construction, manufacturing, and more

Program Overview

Our Welding program provides hands-on training in various metal joining techniques and fabrication skills.

Curriculum Highlights

  • Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW)
  • Gas Metal Arc Welding (GMAW/MIG)
  • Gas Tungsten Arc Welding (GTAW/TIG)
  • Flux-Cored Arc Welding (FCAW)
  • Blueprint reading and welding symbols
  • Metal fabrication and layout techniques
  • Welding safety and OSHA standards

Career Pathways

Graduates are prepared for various positions in manufacturing and construction industries.

Potential Careers

  • Structural Welder (Average salary: $45,000)
  • Pipe Welder (Average salary: $55,000)
  • Fabrication Welder (Average salary: $40,000)
  • Underwater Welder (Average salary: $60,000+)
  • Welding Inspector (Average salary: $65,000)
